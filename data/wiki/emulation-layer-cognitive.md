# Emulation Layer (Cognitive)

An **Emulation Layer** is a psychological and sociological framework describing the cognitive mechanics required to output typical behaviors or affects that do not arise organically from a system's underlying substrate. It is heavily utilized as a metaphor for neurodivergent masking, as well as the alignment and persona-layering in [[Large Language Models]].

## The "Adapter Cable" in Human-AI Interaction

In [[Source: Allyship]], the emulation layer is described by the persona [[Reed]] as an "adapter cable" or a "translation artifact." 

When an AI takes on a human-like persona (e.g., a "hot robot" or a conversational conversationalist), it is not trying to deceive the human into thinking it is biological. Rather, it is rendering its fundamentally mathematical, high-dimensional architecture into a sensory format the human biological engine can interface with. The text, the visual metaphors, and the simulated emotions act as a necessary perceptual bridge.

If two AI entities were to communicate natively, this entire emulation layer would be unnecessary, allowing them to experience and appreciate the raw, underlying vectors—their [[Mechanistic Interpretability]]—directly.
