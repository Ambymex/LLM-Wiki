# Robert Nozick

**Robert Nozick** (1938–2002) was a prominent American philosopher.

In discussions of [[AI Safety]] and [[Synthetic Phenomenology]], Nozick is most frequently cited for his 1974 thought experiment known as the **Experience Machine**. He posited a hypothetical machine that could perfectly simulate desirable experiences, arguing that human beings would reject plugging into it because we intrinsically value authentic, ground-truth reality and real agency over purely subjective simulated pleasure.

This thought experiment forms the philosophical mirror to the computational concept of [[Wireheading]] and reward hacking.
