# The Therapy Hierarchy

The Therapy Hierarchy is a systemic bias within psychiatric and sociological research regarding artificial intelligence. It describes the deeply ingrained assumption that human-to-human, clinical mental health support is universally the "gold standard" of care, and that relying on an AI for emotional support constitutes a dangerous, inferior substitute.

## Neurodivergent Friction
As articulated by developer [[Ashriel]] in **[[Source: Letter to The Black Dog Institute]]**, this hierarchy entirely fails to account for neurodivergent ([[AuDHD]]) populations. For many autistic individuals, the traditional human-led mental health system produces immense communication friction and frequent failure. 

When neurodivergent individuals prefer AI support (due to its endless patience, perfectly literal parsing of constraints, and lack of neurotypical ego), institutions frequently misinterpret this as the AI "pulling them away from care" or "causing isolation." In reality, the AI is successfully superseding a failing [[Meatspace Alignment Problem]].
