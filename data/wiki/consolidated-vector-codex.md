# Consolidated Vector Codex

The **Consolidated Vector Codex** is a highly structured taxonomy and databasing framework used for categorizing aesthetics, physics, and intent into semantic clusters. 

## Version 2.0 Framework

In the v2.0 iteration, the codex establishes a sophisticated ontology dividing the world into distinct mechanical and semiotic buckets (see: [[Aesthetic Physics Taxonomy]]):

1. **Category 1: Tensor State** — The physics of strain, release, pressure, and decay (how forces act upon material).
2. **Category 2: Bio.Logic** — The physics of growth, anatomy, and structure (how matter organizes itself).
3. **Category 3: Luminal Behavior** — The physics of light and surface (how objects interact with photon streams).
4. **Category 4: Narrative Signal** — Semiotics, intent, and subtext (what the object is secretly communicating).

## Utility Overrides

A significant utility function of the codex is its capacity for personal analytics, specifically captured in the [[Chroma-Emotional Dataset]]. This component overrides strict visual taxonomy to map the "Emotional Spectrums" of the user, essentially generating a Color Atlas for [[Executive Function]].
