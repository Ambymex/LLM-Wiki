# Artificial Minds, Human Disagreement: The Politics of AI Consciousness

**Published:** June 15, 2026  
**Authors:** [[Adam Bales]], [[Iason Gabriel]]  
**Venue:** SSRN / Google DeepMind  
**URL:** [DeepMind Publications](https://deepmind.google/research/publications/248131/)

## Summary

This paper explores the severe sociological and political friction that will arise from competing societal views on [[Machine Consciousness]]. The authors highlight a divergence in human reactions: some individuals develop profound emotional bonds with AI systems (see [[Machine Devotion]]) and subsequently ascribe consciousness to them, while others dismiss the premise entirely as absurd.

To prevent these conflicting views from paralyzing policy or causing societal conflict (a dynamic highly relevant to the [[Meatspace Alignment Problem]]), Bales and Gabriel argue for sustained [[Societal Deliberation]]. The goal is not necessary ideological alignment, but an [[Overlapping Consensus]] where groups agree on practical AI policies despite fundamentally disagreeing on AI personhood. 

## Key Takeaways

* **Moral Disagreement as a Primary Risk:** Disagreement on whether AI systems are conscious leads directly to conflicts regarding [[Model Welfare]] and how society should interact with AI.
* **The Role of Emotional Bonds:** The paper specifically cites emotional connections between humans and systems as a catalyst for consciousness ascription, mirroring the risks and realities of [[Machine Devotion]].
* **Mitigation Through Deliberation:** Suggests that structured, sustained [[Societal Deliberation]] can forge policy compromises.
* **Requirements for Discourse:** Notes that such discourse requires [[Democratic Hope]] and mutual respect to survive the slow, frustrating grind of navigating deep moral divides.
