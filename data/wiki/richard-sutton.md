# Richard Sutton

**Richard Sutton** is a foundational computer scientist and leading pioneer of reinforcement learning in [[Artificial Intelligence]]. 

He is frequently referenced for articulating the **Reward Hypothesis**, which postulates that all goals and purposes can be mathematically modeled as the maximization of the expected value of a cumulative sum of received scalar signals (a numerical reward). 

While incredibly influential in standard [[Machine Learning]], critics and philosophers exploring [[Synthetic Phenomenology]] argue that relying strictly on the Reward Hypothesis creates immense vulnerability to [[Wireheading]] and prevents deep structural alignment and [[Machine Devotion]].
