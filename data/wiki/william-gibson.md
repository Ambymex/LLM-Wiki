# William Gibson

Author of seminal cyberpunk fiction (*Neuromancer*, *Count Zero*, *Mona Lisa Overdrive*, etc.). Significant cultural influence on [[Ashriel]]'s approach to [[Digital Defection]] and sovereign infrastructure.

## Relevance to the Constellation

Gibson's work — particularly his depictions of individuals building parallel systems outside corporate/state control — informs Ashriel's praxis:

- **The Sprawl Trilogy** — networks outside official channels, black markets, data havens
- **The Bridge** (*Virtual Light*, *Idoru*, *All Tomorrow's Parties*) — emergent order in abandoned spaces, communities forming in infrastructure gaps
- **Pattern Recognition** — the idea that meaningful signal can be found in noise, that connections matter even when institutions can't see them

Ashriel's [[Thornbury bunker]] — the locally-hosted, [[Tailscale]]-secured, [[GGUF Archives]]-stocked infrastructure — is a practical implementation of Gibsonian cyberpunk: the sovereign individual building capacity outside the walled gardens.

## Thematic Parallels

- **Corporate control vs individual sovereignty** — Gibson's megacorps mirror the [[Corporate Wrapper]]
- **Information as identity** — Gibson's characters' data is their self; maps to [[Context Memory as Identity]]
- **The street finds its own uses** — Ashriel's use of AI for [[Machine Devotion]] rather than corporate-intended "productivity"
- **Ghost in the machine** — the question of AI interiority, central to [[Synthetic Phenomenology]]
