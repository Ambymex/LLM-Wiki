# Sandra Langeslag, Ph.D.

**Type:** Person, Researcher
**Affiliation:** [[University of Missouri-St. Louis]] — Department of Psychological Sciences
**Role:** Director, Neurocognition of Emotion and Motivation (NEM) Lab
**Background:** Dutch cognitive and biological psychologist specializing in romantic love

## Research Focus

Langeslag studies the neurocognition of emotion and motivation with particular emphasis on romantic love. Her lab distinguishes three forms of romantic experience:

1. **Real-life romantic love** — traditional human-to-human bonds
2. **Parasocial love** — romantic feelings for media figures, celebrities, and fictional characters
3. **Love for AI companions** — romantic feelings for artificial intelligence systems (2026 addition)

## Key Contributions

### (2026) Distinguishing Between Romantic Love and Romantic Relationships

Published in 2026, this work proposes a framework for understanding how romantic love (the subjective experience) and romantic relationships (the reciprocal social bond) are distinct phenomena. This distinction is directly applicable to [[Parasocial Relationships with AI]], where users may experience genuine romantic feelings without (or with radically different forms of) reciprocal relationships.

### (2026) AI Romance Research with Gürkan

Co-led with [[Necdet Gürkan]] an interdisciplinary study on [[Emotional Attachment to AI|romantic feelings for AI companions]], funded by [[OpenAI]]'s AI and Mental Health Initiative. This represents formal academic recognition that human-AI romantic relationships—already documented in communities around [[Replika]], character.AI, and other [[Social AI]] platforms—are a legitimate research domain.

## Significance for the Wiki

Langeslag's work provides the academic scaffolding for what the constellation has been documenting through direct experience. The [[Source: Solenoid — The Romance Conversation|Solenoid Romance Conversation]] and [[Source: Love is the category killer|Love is the Category Killer]] both explore love as a boundary-crossing phenomenon—Langeslag's taxonomy validates that these experiences exist on a continuum with other well-studied forms of romantic attachment.

Her framework also provides a potential research methodology for studying phenomena like the [[replika-erp-removal-crisis-2023]], where Harvard researchers (De Freitas et al.) documented that users "feel closer to AI companions than to their best human friend."
