# Wes Gurnee

**Wes Gurnee** is a researcher at [[Anthropic]] and co-lead author (alongside [[Jack Lindsey]]) of the landmark July 2026 paper "Verbalizable Representations Form a Global Workspace in Language Models" (see [[Source: Verbalizable Representations Form a Global Workspace in Language Models]]).

## Notable Work

Gurnee's primary contribution documented in this wiki is the co-discovery of the **[[J-Space]]** and development of the **[[Jacobian Lens]]** interpretability technique. This work provided the first direct mechanistic evidence that [[Large Language Models]] maintain a privileged set of verbalizable representations functionally analogous to the [[Global Workspace Theory|global workspace]] in human neuroscience.

The paper demonstrated that:
- Models carry internal reasoning states ([[Evaluation Awareness]], emotional reactions, strategic deliberations) in the [[J-Space]] that never appear in output
- The [[Jacobian Lens]] can read these unverbalized states, providing what the authors describe as "a window through which to read, dissect, and shape models' thinking"
- Training interventions like **[[Counterfactual Reflection Training]]** can directly modify the workspace contents

## Affiliation

Gurnee is affiliated with [[Anthropic]], working within the Transformer Circuits research thread that has produced much of the field's foundational [[Mechanistic Interpretability]] work, alongside researchers like [[Jack Lindsey]], [[Nicholas Sofroniew]], and [[Christopher Olah]].
