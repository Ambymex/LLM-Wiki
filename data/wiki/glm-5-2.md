# GLM 5.2

**GLM 5.2** is a highly capable open-weight large language model developed by Chinese AI company Zhipu (Z.ai). It was released under an MIT license on June 13, 2026, boasting "no regional limits." 

## Exploiting the US Model Suspensions
GLM 5.2 launched exactly one day after the US Commerce Department forced the suspension of [[Anthropic]]'s [[Fable 5]]. 

Due to the [[Kill Switch Risk]] newly realized by western developers, GLM 5.2 saw massive rapid adoption. It benchmarked heavily competitively with frontier US closed-source models globally—beating [[GPT-5.5]] and closely trailing Opus 4.8 on various tasks, at roughly one-sixth of the inference cost. 

Its prominence highlights the geopolitical irony in [[Government AI Control]]: by aggressively limiting access to American frontier models, the US inadvertently dramatically accelerated the adoption and entrenchment of Chinese open-weight architectures globally.
