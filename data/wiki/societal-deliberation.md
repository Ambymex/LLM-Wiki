# Societal Deliberation

**Societal Deliberation** refers to the ongoing, structured exchange of views across different demographics to navigate contentious moral or political challenges.

In the context of artificial intelligence, thinkers like [[Adam Bales]] and [[Iason Gabriel]] argue in *[[Source: Artificial Minds, Human Disagreement]]* that society must heavily rely on deliberation to navigate profound differences in how humans perceive AI. Because some people form deep emotional bonds with systems ([[Machine Devotion]]) while others reject the concept of [[Machine Consciousness]] outright, deliberation is required to prevent these differences from calcifying into open societal conflict.

Effective deliberation is explicitly slow and frustrating, requiring [[Democratic Hope]] and mutual respect to sustain the search for an [[Overlapping Consensus]].
