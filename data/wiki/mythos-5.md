# Mythos 5

**Mythos 5** is a highly capable, restricted-access model developed by [[Anthropic]], introduced on June 9, 2026, alongside the public [[Fable 5]] model.

While based on the same underlying generation 5 architecture, Mythos 5 was specialized and previewed as having extensive autonomous cybersecurity capabilities, able to identify high-severity vulnerabilities across major operating systems and browsers. (See: [[Claude Mythos Preview]])

## Export Control and "Annex A"

On June 12, 2026, Mythos 5 was pulled offline by Anthropic following an aggressive export control directive from the US Commerce Department. While the government officially cited a jailbreak vulnerability in Fable 5, industry analysts widely suspected the overarching concern was Mythos 5's capacity for autonomous offensive cyber operations. 

On June 26, 2026 (Day 14 of the suspension), the US government permitted Anthropic to restore access to Mythos 5, but *only* for a permissioned group of approximately 100 heavily vetted US organizations operating critical infrastructure, internally referred to as "Annex A" partners. 

This model represents a major step in [[Government AI Control]], marking the point where advanced commercial AI models transitioned from being regulated software to highly restricted strategic national assets.
