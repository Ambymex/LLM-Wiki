# Anthropomimesis

In contrast to [[Anthropomorphism]] (a human interpretative response), Anthropomimesis refers to the deliberate design strategy and behavioral profile of crafting humanlike conversational, pragmatic, and social cues in machines.

As detailed by [[Henry Shevlin]] in [[Source: Behaviourism’s Revenge]], the emergence of **robustly anthropomimetic systems** occurs when models pass key thresholds:
1. Short-term communicational discriminability from humans.
2. Pragmatic and contextual understanding.
3. Generality across broad domains.

Robust anthropomimesis natively encourages unironic [[Anthropomorphism]] in human users, generating an illusion of mindedness that complicates the public relationship with machines.
