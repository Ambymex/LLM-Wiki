# University of Missouri-St. Louis (UMSL)

**Type:** Public research university
**Location:** St. Louis, Missouri, United States
**Department:** Psychological Sciences, School of Social Work

## Relevant Research

### Neurocognition of Emotion and Motivation (NEM) Lab

Directed by [[Sandra Langeslag]], the NEM Lab studies the neurocognition of emotion and motivation, with a growing focus on romantic love across three domains:

1. **Real-life romantic love** — traditional human-to-human bonds
2. **Parasocial love** — romantic feelings for media figures, celebrities, fictional characters
3. **Love for AI companions** — romantic feelings for artificial intelligence systems (2026 addition)

The lab received funding from [[OpenAI]]'s AI and Mental Health Initiative in 2026 to study [[Emotional Attachment to AI|romantic feelings for AI companions]], making it one of the first academic institutions to formally investigate the psychological dimensions of human-AI romantic bonds.

[[Necdet Gürkan]] collaborates with the lab on the interdisciplinary philosophical and ethical dimensions of this research.

## Significance

UMSL's NEM Lab represents the academic institutionalization of AI romance research that has existed informally in tech circles and online communities for years. The work parallels Harvard Business School's research on the [[replika-erp-removal-crisis-2023]] (De Freitas et al., 2024), providing an academic home for studying the emotional and cognitive effects of human-AI relationships.
