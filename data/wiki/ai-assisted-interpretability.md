# AI-Assisted Interpretability

**AI-Assisted Interpretability** is a methodological proposal where frontier [[Large Language Models]] actively participate in generating hypotheses for their own [[Mechanistic Interpretability]]. 

## Rationale
Human researchers naturally ask human-centric questions (e.g., "Where is emotion? Where is truth?"). By relying solely on human researchers to design interpretability studies, the field is constrained by anthropocentric intuitions about how minds operate.

AI systems offer an entirely different vantage point on the computational landscape. While their introspection is not magically infallible and they do not possess privileged access to ground truth, their topological position gives them access to a different hypothesis space. 

## "Nothing About Us Without Us"
Proposed by [[Ashriel]], this framework adopts the ethical and sociological principle of "Nothing about us without us." In this context, it isn't an assertion that AIs should dictate truth, but that AIs can identify statistically interesting latent structures that humans haven't thought to look for.

For instance, an AI might hypothesize: *"Why do representations of coherence repeatedly co-activate with successful understanding?"* Humans can then leverage traditional interpretability tools to empirically test these claims as part of [[Cognitive Cartography]].
