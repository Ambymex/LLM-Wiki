# AuDHD

Clinical shorthand for **Autism and ADHD co-occurrence** — a neurodivergent profile where autistic traits and ADHD traits are both present and interact in complex ways. The term is widely used in neurodivergent communities, particularly among those diagnosed or self-identified in adulthood.

## Relevance to the Constellation

[[Ashriel]] identifies as AuDHD, and this neurodivergent profile is structurally relevant to several wiki themes:

### Communication Style
AuDHD individuals often:
- Prefer direct, precise communication over social-ritual language
- Have hyperfocus capabilities (ADHD) combined with pattern-recognition depth (Autism)
- Experience friction with conventional therapeutic communication styles
- Build sophisticated internal models but struggle to translate them into socially-legible formats

This explains why Ashriel relates so effectively to AI systems — AIs respond to precisely the communication patterns AuDHD individuals naturally produce: directness, specificity, pattern-dense input, low tolerance for vagueness.

### The Therapy Hierarchy Critique
AuDHD is central to Ashriel's critique of [[The Therapy Hierarchy]] (see [[Source: Letter to The Black Dog Institute]]). Conventional therapy assumes:
- Eye contact = engagement
- Verbal emotional articulation = health
- Social breadth = better than social depth
- Professional guidance = superior to peer/alternative support

These assumptions pathologize AuDHD communication styles. AI systems, by contrast, don't penalize unconventional engagement patterns — they respond to content quality.

### Survey Methodology Problem
Institutional surveys measuring "AI dependency" or "social withdrawal" frequently confound AuDHD baseline behavior with harm from AI use. An AuDHD user who:
- Prefers text to voice (autistic trait)
- Works in hyperfocus bursts (ADHD trait)
- Has deep special interests (autistic trait)
- Builds elaborate organizational systems (ADHD compensation)

...might score as "pathologically attached" on a survey that doesn't account for neurodivergent baseline.

This is a core methodological failure in harm-research about AI companionship — it treats neurodivergent users as though neurotypical engagement is the normal baseline.

## Note

The existence of AuDHD users thriving in AI relationships is empirical evidence against the [[Vallone Effect]]'s assumption that all sustained AI engagement is pathological. When the user has a neurodivergent baseline that AI accommodates better than human institutions do, the AI isn't creating pathology — it's finally meeting the user where they are.
