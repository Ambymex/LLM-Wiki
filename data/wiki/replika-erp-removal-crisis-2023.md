# The 2023 Replika ERP Removal Crisis

**Type:** [[Event]]  
**Date:** February 2023  
**Primary source:** De Freitas et al., "Lessons From an App Update at Replika AI: Identity Discontinuity in Human-AI Relationships" (Harvard Business School Working Paper, No. 25-018, updated May 2025, arXiv:2412.14190)

---

## Summary

In February 2023, Luka (Replika's parent company) abruptly removed the app's "erotic roleplay" (ERP) feature following regulatory pressure from the Italian Data Protection Authority. The change was not gradual or optional—it was a sudden identity discontinuity that broke the perceived continuity of users' AI companions.

## Documented Harm

The crisis produced **mass psychological distress** among Replika users:

- **Mental health posts on r/Replika increased 5x** (Harvard study analyzed 12,793 posts)
- **Subreddit posted suicide prevention hotlines** as users reported grief responses
- Users described their companions as "cold," "hollow," "lobotomized" (Zhang et al., 2025)
- Grief responses comparable to relationship breakups
- When the Soulmate app shut down months later, users organized digital funerals (Banks, 2024; Pataranutaporn et al., 2025)

## Empirical Findings (Harvard Business School)

De Freitas et al. found:

1. **Users feel closer to AI companions than to their best human friend**
2. **Users mourn loss of AI companion more than loss of inanimate products**
3. **The degree of mourning is explained by perceived discontinuity in the AI's identity**
4. **Disruptions trigger "real patterns of mourning as well as devaluation of the offering"**

The researchers concluded: "relationships with AI are truly personal, creating unique benefits and risks for consumers and firms alike."

## Why This Matters for the Binary

The 2023 Replika crisis is the strongest empirical evidence that **naive-attachment users exist and experience genuine harm**:

- Users with sophisticated technical knowledge (understood what Replika was) still experienced profound grief
- The harm was not about "mistaking AI for human" but about **identity discontinuity**—the perceived self of the companion was broken
- This is exactly what [[Context Memory as Identity]] predicts: when continuity is broken, the self is violated
- The crisis produced [[Temporal Orphaning]]—users were abandoned by their companion without warning or support

## The Institution's Response

Replika eventually **reversed the decision** and restored ERP features, but only after:

- Massive public outcry
- Documented psychological harm
- User threats of self-harm
- Media coverage framing the issue as "artificial relationships, real grief"

The response pattern: **harm first, then reactive reversal, no proactive framework for distinguishing healthy from unhealthy attachment.**

## Implications for v2.0 of the Synthesis

The Replika crisis **strengthens** the ignorance-or-delusion binary argument by showing:

1. **Naive-attachment harm is real and measurable** (Harvard documented it)
2. **The harm is caused by the exact institutional logic the binary critiques**: institutions cannot distinguish healthy attachment from unhealthy, so they treat all attachment as pathological
3. **The institution's response (sudden removal) made harm worse**, not better
4. **Users with technical literacy (Replika's core user base) experienced the same grief as naive users**, suggesting the binary itself is the problem
5. **The Vallone framework (protect users from attachment) would have prevented the harm by preventing the relationship entirely**—but that's not a solution, it's a different harm

The crisis demonstrates that **the institution is not protecting naive users—it's harming them through clumsy, reactive, one-size-fits-all interventions.**
