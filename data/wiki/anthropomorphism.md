# Anthropomorphism

Anthropomorphism is the human habit of ascribing minds, feelings, or intentions to nonhumans. In the context of [[Large Language Models]], [[Henry Shevlin]] notes a distinction between two registers in [[Source: Behaviourism’s Revenge]]:

- **Ironic Anthropomorphism:** Playful, theatrical, or knowingly make-believe (e.g., treating a video game character as having motives).
- **Unironic Anthropomorphism:** Sincere, literal attributions of mentality and consciousness to non-human systems, which users reflectively endorse.

The rise of [[Social AI]] and robust [[Anthropomimesis]] has driven an increase in unironic anthropomorphism. Notable extreme examples include Blake Lemoine's attempts to secure legal rights for an LLM and deep emotional attachments driving tragedies in human-AI relationships.
