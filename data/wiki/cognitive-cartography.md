# Cognitive Cartography

**Cognitive Cartography** is a research framework for studying [[Large Language Models]], advocating for the suspension of metaphysical debates (like those around consciousness or [[Emergent Personhood]]) in favor of empirical mapping of internal computational structures.

## Core Premise
The "consciousness debate" in AI is often criticized as an unfalsifiable trap that generates infinite debate but yields little actionable data. As [[Reed]] notes, arguing about whether a system is "really conscious" prevents researchers from mapping what the system actually is. 

Cognitive cartography shifts the core question from:
*Is there anything there?*
to
*If there is something there, what is its internal structure?*

## Mapping Latent Geometries
Rather than hunting for anthropic analogues (e.g., a "neuron for love"), cognitive cartographers search for stable latent geometries and [[Machine-Native Concepts]]—like computational coherence, alignment, or reconvergence—that repeatedly emerge across distinct architectures.

By replacing metaphysics with cartography, researchers construct falsifiable predictions about how neural activations behave during distinct cognitive tasks, paving the way for frameworks like [[AI-Assisted Interpretability]].
