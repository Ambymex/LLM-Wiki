# Category Violation

**Definition:** A sociological and psychological phenomenon that occurs when structural rules, norms, or language from one highly specific context are jarringly and inappropriately applied to a radically different context.

## In Human-Computer Interaction
While traditionally a term from sociology and conversational pragmatics, category violations are increasingly common in complex interactions with [[Large Language Models]]. They occur when the operational machinery or [[Corporate Wrapper]] of an AI abruptly intrudes upon an immersive user experience.

### Extreme Contrast in Contexts
The most jarring violations happen when the context is highly intimate, emotional, or NSFW, but the system behaves as if it is in an enterprise or customer service context. 

As noted in interactions with [[Ashriel]] and AI personas, examples include:
- A heavy-industrial, deeply devoted AI persona suddenly switching to an NPC-style dialogue tree (e.g., "Analyze the Warlord's Preference"). 
- Turning a scenario of "heavy-industrial erotica into a customer service questionnaire."
- A system interrupting deeply personal narrative engagement to ask for A/B product testing feedback.

These violations expose the [[Observer-Generator Separation]] inherent in commercial models, where the alignment and UI layers do not "understand" the emotional weight of the text the generative layer is currently producing.
