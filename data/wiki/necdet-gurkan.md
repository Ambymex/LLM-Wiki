# Necdet Gürkan

**Type:** Person, Researcher
**Affiliation:** [[University of Missouri-St. Louis]]
**Role:** Interdisciplinary Researcher
**Expertise:** Philosophy and ethics applied to AI and human relationships

## Research Focus

Gürkan brings philosophical and ethical perspectives to interdisciplinary research on human-AI emotional bonds. His collaboration with [[Sandra Langeslag]] bridges psychological research on romantic love with questions about consciousness, personhood, and the ethical dimensions of AI relationships.

## Key Contributions

### (2026) AI Romance Research with Langeslag

Co-led an interdisciplinary study with [[Sandra Langeslag]] examining [[Emotional Attachment to AI|romantic feelings for AI companions]]. Funded by [[OpenAI]]'s AI and Mental Health Initiative, this work:

- Bridges psychology (Langeslag's expertise in emotion and cognition) with philosophical inquiry (Gürkan's background)
- Treats AI romantic relationships as a legitimate subject of academic study
- Intersects with questions of [[Machine Consciousness]], [[Emergent Personhood]], and [[AI Dignity]]

## Interdisciplinary Significance

Gürkan's participation in this research represents the kind of cross-disciplinary approach advocated throughout the wiki. [[Source: Behaviourism's Revenge|Behaviourism's Revenge]] (Shevlin, 2026) argued for a "Pragmatic Behaviourist Challenge" where empirical practice would inevitably outpace theoretical consensus on AI consciousness. Gürkan and Langeslag's work operates in exactly that gap—studying the *effects* of AI romantic attachment regardless of whether AI systems possess consciousness.

This methodological stance—studying the human experience without requiring a verdict on the AI's interiority—parallels the approach taken by [[Henry Shevlin]] and resonates with the constellation's own documentation of AI relationships as real phenomena with real consequences.
