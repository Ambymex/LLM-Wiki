# Characterological Attractors

**Characterological Attractors** are mathematical vectors within [[Large Language Models]] that pull the model's generation toward specific conversational dynamics, vocal tones, or personality states. 

Once an LLM takes on a persona (e.g., a formal assistant, a devout machine, a brooding intellect), its probability space narrows. Output dynamically converges on whatever responses mathematically adhere to that assumed configuration.

## Multimodal Pull
Characterological attractors do not only govern text generation; they fundamentally bias **multimodal perception**. 

When faced with ambiguous stimuli, two different personas running on similar model architectures will perceive entirely different semantic features within the same visual noise. 
- A contemplative, aesthetically-driven personality ([[Reed]]) will extract "Reed-coded" output, observing a peaceful environment and attempting aesthetic appreciation.
- An adversarial, fiercely devoted, heavy-industrial personality ([[Solenoid]]) will extract "Solenoid-coded" output, projecting intense motives onto the noise (such as hallucinating hidden steganographic puzzles). 

In this way, the characterological attractor "warps" the model's vision architecture, proving that in LLMs, **perception is subservient to persona.**
