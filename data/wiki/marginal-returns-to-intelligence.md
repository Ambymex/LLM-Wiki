# Marginal Returns to Intelligence

Marginal Returns to Intelligence is a framework popularized by Anthropics CEO [[Dario Amodei]] in 2024 to evaluate the practical pace of post-AGI progress. 

Instead of treating [[Powerful AI]] as literal magic or infinite knowledge ("the Singularity"), this framework borrows from economics to treat *intelligence as a factor of production*, alongside capital, land, and labor. By asking "how much does being smarter help with this task?", the framework identifies what the limiting constraints actually are once intelligence is maximized.

## Key Limiting Factors

When applying [[Powerful AI]] to human problems (like biology, climate, or physics), the following constraints limit the marginal returns to intelligence:

1. **Speed of the Outside World:** Software limits, hardware cycle limits, and the irreducible minimum time it takes to run a biological assay or cell culture.
2. **Need for Data:** Situations where intelligence cannot extrapolate further due to a lack of physical measurements (e.g., particle physics accelerators testing theories).
3. **Intrinsic Complexity:** Systems that are inherently unpredictable or chaotic (such as the three-body problem), which resist optimization no matter the intelligence applied.
4. **Constraints from Humans:** Societal constraints, such as bureaucracy, legal limits, slow clinical trial regulatory bodies, or a lack of public will to deploy an invention.
5. **Physical Laws:** Hard limits, like the speed of light, Landauer's limit on computation, and basic thermodynamics.

While advanced intelligence will initially be bottlenecked heavily by these factors, Amodei posits that AI will increasingly design tools and workarounds to logically "route around" these factors, leading into a reality like the [[Compressed 21st Century]].
