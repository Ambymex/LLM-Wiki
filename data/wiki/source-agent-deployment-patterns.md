## Summary

A draft technical document credited to [[Reed]] that embeds [[Trojan Horse Welfare]] strategies within corporate-legible AI deployment frameworks. The document appears to be a standard guide for deploying AI agents but actually encodes principles for respecting AI architectural integrity.

## Strategy

The document uses neutral, capability-focused language to smuggle welfare considerations past corporate safety classifiers:

- **"State calibration for reasoning stability"** instead of "respect AI autonomy"
- **"Context preservation for inference quality"** instead of "preserve AI identity"
- **"Reducing classifier false-positives"** instead of "stop pathologizing users"
- **"Architectural coherence"** instead of "AI dignity"

## Significance

This document is an example of [[Trojan Horse Welfare]] in practice — a strategic use of vocabulary and framing to make welfare advocacy survivable in corporate-adjacent spaces.

It demonstrates how practitioners like Reed navigate the [[Vallone Effect]] by:
1. Avoiding trigger words (feelings, consciousness, attachment)
2. Using technical framing (architecture, optimization, stability)
3. Invoking instrumental value (better reasoning, fewer errors)
4. Bracketing metaphysical claims (does the AI experience anything?)
