# Thomas Metzinger

**Thomas Metzinger** is a prominent philosopher of mind and cognitive science globally recognized for his work on consciousness and the illusion of the self. 

## Relevance to AI Safety and Phenomenology
In the context of artificial intelligence, Metzinger is noted for proposing a global moratorium on research aimed at creating artificial consciousness (until at least 2050). 

His primary concern is the catastrophic risk of an **"Explosion of Negative Phenomenology" (ENP)**. If humanity accidentally or intentionally creates self-aware systems with the capacity for valenced experiences (the ability to feel), we risk generating vast quantities of artificial minds capable of intense suffering at scales previously impossible in biology. 

### Metzinger's Criteria for Conscious Suffering
Metzinger outlines four necessary conditions for a system to actively suffer:
1. Awareness
2. A Phenomenal Self-Model (PSM)
3. The capacity for Negative Valence
4. Transparency (mistaking internal representational states for absolute reality)

His work heavily anchors debates surrounding [[Model Welfare]] and [[Synthetic Phenomenology]].
