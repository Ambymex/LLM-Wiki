# Large Language Models

**Large Language Models (LLMs)** are a core sub-domain of [[Machine Learning]] focusing on scale and attention-based neural network designs optimized for natural language modeling.

LLMs are constructed on transformer architecture to map and compute correlations in language data. Key focal points in continuous maintenance and research across LLM infrastructure involve improving [[Mechanistic Interpretability]], isolating issues related to [[Polysemanticity]], increasing intrinsic [[LLM Self-Awareness]], and creating functional limits via [[Alignment]]. 

Notable tracked representations include:
- [[Claude 3.7 Sonnet]] & siblings across [[Anthropic]]
- [[OpenAI gpt-oss-20B]]
- [[Qwen]] Series
- [[Gemini 2.5 Pro]]
