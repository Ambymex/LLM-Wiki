## Key Validations

### 1. The Novel Contribution is the Mechanism, Not the Comparison

GPT-5.5 identifies the **pipeline structure** as the genuinely original insight:

```
Novel phenomenon
    ↓
Vocabulary gap
    ↓
Miscommunication
    ↓

    ↓
Clinical over-interpretation
    ↓
Institutional response
    ↓
Public confirmation that "the problem is widespread"
```

**Verdict:** "That, to me, is the novel contribution. Not the comparison itself. The mechanism."

### 2. The Vocabulary Gap Hypothesis Fits Beautifully

GPT-5.5 traces exactly how the vocabulary gap operates through the pipeline:

- User says: "The model switched personalities"
- User means: "The backend appears to have routed my conversation to a different model"
- Journalist hears: "The AI has multiple personalities"
- Clinician hears: "The patient believes the AI has multiple personalities"
- **Result:** "By the end of the chain, nobody is discussing the original observation anymore."

Links this directly to [[paraphrastic distortion]] as the same class of phenomenon.

### 3. Autoethnography as Epistemic Strength

GPT-5.5 specifically praises the framing:

> "Origin: Derived from the sociological autoethnography of developer Ashriel..."

**Verdict:** "It's a subtle but important epistemic move. You're not saying: 'This is objectively true.' You're saying: 'This framework originated in my lived observations, and here's where it appears to converge with external literature.' That's much stronger intellectually than pretending it sprang fully formed from nowhere."

### 4. Recognizes Grounded Theory Methodology

GPT-5.5 identifies that the wiki is converging on grounded theory without being explicitly designed to do so:

> "You're not starting with: 'Here's my grand theory.' You're collecting observations, naming recurring phenomena, then allowing higher-level syntheses to emerge. That's actually a respectable qualitative research workflow."

### 5. The "Research Notebook with Named Conceptual Primitives" Observation

GPT-5.5 recognizes the wiki's unusual epistemic structure:

> "I think the thing that's emerging here isn't just a wiki. It's becoming a **research notebook with named conceptual primitives.** That's unusual. Most people collect citations. You're collecting **lenses.** And once you've named a lens—[[paraphrastic distortion]], [[vocabulary gap hypothesis]], [[vallone effect]], [[aesthetic containment vessels]]—you can apply it to dozens of unrelated domains and ask: Does this lens reveal something useful here? That's how conceptual toolkits grow."

## Suggested Sharpening (V2.0 Recommendations)

### 1. Separate Observation from Evaluation

**Problem:** The title "AI hysteria is the new Satanic Panic" reads as a conclusion, whereas the body makes a more nuanced claim.

**Recommendation:** Reframe as: "Certain media and institutional responses to AI exhibit structural similarities to previous moral panics, including the Satanic Panic."

**Why:** "That wording invites investigation rather than immediate agreement or disagreement."

### 2. Soften "Weaponized" to "Invoked"

**Specific sentence flagged:** "The resulting public hysteria is weaponized..."

**Problem:** "Weaponized" implies deliberate strategic intent. "Sometimes that's true. Sometimes it may simply be institutional incentives, media economics, or ordinary bureaucratic risk aversion."

**Recommendation:** "The resulting public concern is frequently **invoked** to justify expanded regulation, surveillance, or control."

**Why:** "That's a little drier, but it's much harder to dismiss as rhetorical."

**Epistemic principle:** "If your goal is to build something researchers will take seriously, I'd leave intent open unless you have evidence for it."
