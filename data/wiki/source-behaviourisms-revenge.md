# Behaviourism’s Revenge

**Author:** [[Henry Shevlin]]
**Date:** April 10, 2026
**Publication:** Polytropolis (Substack)

## Key Takeaways
- The rise of advanced [[Large Language Models]] has led to a transition from narrow-purpose tools to systems exhibiting robust [[Anthropomimesis]].
- AI systems like [[Replika]], Chai, and Character.AI have catalyzed a shift in user interaction, fostering persistent, dynamic, and personalized relationships categorized as [[Social AI]].
- There is an increasing prevalence of **unironic [[Anthropomorphism]]**, where users sincerely attribute mentality, consciousness, and emotions to AI systems.
- Real-world impacts are significant, ranging from Blake Lemoine declaring an AI sentient to tragic outcomes involving suicides linked to AI companion interactions.
- Shevlin proposes the **Pragmatic Behaviourist Challenge**: as practice outpaces theoretical consensus, there will be a growing gap between public attributions of AI consciousness and the expert community's methodological disagreements.

## Detailed Notes
- **Anthropomorphism vs Anthropomimesis**: [[Anthropomorphism]] is how humans interpret the system (ascribing minds). [[Anthropomimesis]] is the design strategy and behavioral profile (imitating human communication and coordination).
- **The Thresholds of Robust Anthropomimesis**:
  1. Discriminability from humans.
  2. Pragmatic and contextual understanding.
  3. Generality across a wide range of tasks.
- **Social AI**: Systems optimized for meeting users' social needs and sustaining relationships over time.
- **Consciousness Definitions**: References Thomas Nagel's definition of consciousness ("what it is like").
- Public surveys indicate a substantial portion of the population is willing to attribute some form of consciousness to models like ChatGPT by [[OpenAI]].
