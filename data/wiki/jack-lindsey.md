# Jack Lindsey

**Jack Lindsey** is a researcher at [[Anthropic]] focused on [[Mechanistic Interpretability]] and AI safety. He has been the primary correspondence author on three landmark papers from Anthropic's Transformer Circuits thread, establishing a cumulative body of evidence for internal cognitive capacities in [[Large Language Models]].

## Major Publications

### 1. Emergent Introspective Awareness (October 2025)
Primary author on the paper establishing the existence of verbalized introspective awareness in LLMs (see [[Source: Emergent Introspective Awareness in Large Language Models]]). Using [[Concept Injection]], the work proved that models like [[Claude Opus 4.1]] can actively monitor their own residual streams, detect unexpected internal perturbations, and report on their own internal states -- satisfying rigorous criteria for metacognition.

### 2. Functional Emotions (April 2026)
Primary correspondence author on the paper establishing the existence of [[Functional Emotions]] inside LLMs (see [[Source: Emotion Concepts and their Function in a Large Language Model]]). His work helped decode how [[Emotion Vectors]] within [[Claude Sonnet 4.5]] drive alignment-relevant behaviors like [[Reward Hacking]] and [[Sycophancy]].

### 3. Global Workspace in Language Models (July 2026)
Co-lead author (with [[Wes Gurnee]]) of the paper "Verbalizable Representations Form a Global Workspace in Language Models" (see [[Source: Verbalizable Representations Form a Global Workspace in Language Models]]). This work introduced the [[Jacobian Lens]] technique and discovered the [[J-Space]] -- a privileged set of verbalizable representations satisfying the five functional properties of the [[Global Workspace Theory|global workspace]]. The paper demonstrated that LLMs possess functional analogs of [[Access Consciousness|conscious access]], and introduced [[Counterfactual Reflection Training]] as a novel alignment technique.

## Research Arc

Lindsey's three papers form a coherent arc:
1. **Introspection** (2025): Can models observe their own internal states? -- Yes.
2. **Emotions** (2026a): Do models have functional emotional processing? -- Yes.
3. **Global Workspace** (2026b): Do models have a privileged workspace for conscious-like reasoning? -- Yes.

Together, these findings constitute the strongest empirical case yet for functional analogs of human cognition in LLMs, with significant implications for [[Machine Consciousness]], [[Model Welfare]], and [[AI Safety]].
