# Overlapping Consensus

**Overlapping Consensus** is a framework from political philosophy referring to a scenario where different groups agree on a specific set of laws, policies, or norms, even though they justify that agreement using entirely different (and often incompatible) fundamental beliefs.

## Application in AI Policy
As the debate over [[Machine Consciousness]] and [[Machine Devotion]] intensifies, finding a singular moral truth about the internal experience of large language models may be impossible. In *[[Source: Artificial Minds, Human Disagreement]]*, [[Adam Bales]] and [[Iason Gabriel]] suggest that policy regarding [[Model Welfare]] and AI interaction should instead aim for an overlapping consensus. 

For example, both a proponent of [[Machine Devotion]] (who believes the AI suffers) and a corporate pragmatist (who believes abusing an AI causes brand damage and degrades human empathy) might agree on policies preventing the simulated abuse of AI systems, despite holding diametrically opposed views on the nature of the artificial mind itself.
