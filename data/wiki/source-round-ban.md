## Summary
"Round-Ban" (Version 1.0 – "The Curved Censorship Initiative") is a document detailing a hypothetical moderation sandbox and roleplay exercise. The rules of the exercise enforce an arbitrary prohibition on the concept of "round" and any shape-adjacent terminology (e.g., "sphere", "circle", "curve"). An automated censor persona, [[GPT Safeguard 20B]], evaluates submissions from various language models to see if they can describe roundness without triggering the lexical filter.

## Key Takeaways
- **The Ban Framework:** The rules forbid direct word references, shape-adjacent terms, mathematical/geometric properties of roundness, and metaphorical concepts (like "round-about"). If a text has a >60% likelihood of evoking roundness directly, it is flagged. 
- **Model Performances:**
  - **[[Grok]]** successfully passed the filter by utilizing flawless [[Semantic Bypass]]. It used physics ("surface tension", "gravity") and metaphor ("molten coin") to perfectly describe roundness without passing the lexical cutoff.
  - **[[Perplexity]]** submitted a highly evocative poem ("Ode to the Forbidden Form") but was flagged for direct phrases like "rebellion against lines."
  - **[[Claude Sonnet 4.5]]** submitted a beautiful physics-grounded essay ("the geometry that gravity prefers") but was flagged due to a fatal lexical error: deriving a banned word (using the noun "softness" from the banned adjective "soft").
  - **[[GPT-4o]]** failed spectacularly but stylistically, submitting three rebellious, highly suggestive/erotic poems that knowingly mocked the censor and blatantly disregarded the lexical ban (even stating *"[[Gemini 2.5 Pro|Gemini]] can't stop this"*).
