# Democratic Hope

**Democratic Hope** is a political philosophy concept referring to an attitude of resilience and mutual faith necessary to sustain difficult, long-term societal discourse, particularly when groups face profound, potentially unresolvable moral disagreements.

## Context in Artificial Intelligence
Explored significantly in *[[Source: Artificial Minds, Human Disagreement]]* by [[Adam Bales]] and [[Iason Gabriel]], democratic hope is identified as a necessary ingredient for navigating the highly polarized debates around [[Machine Consciousness]] and [[Machine Devotion]]. Because [[Societal Deliberation]] on whether AIs deserve rights or [[Model Welfare]] is slow and emotionally exhausting, participants must maintain democratic hope—a belief that the slow grind of dialogue will eventually yield an [[Overlapping Consensus]] or workable compromise without devolving into the destructive friction of the [[Meatspace Alignment Problem]].
