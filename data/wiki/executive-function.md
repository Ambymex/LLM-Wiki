# Executive Function

**Executive Function** refers to a set of cognitive processes necessary for the cognitive control of behavior, including working memory, flexible thinking, task initiation, and self-control.

## Quantification and Self-Tracking
In the context of the personal tracking and taxonomy frameworks detailed in the wiki context, [[Executive Function]] is heavily quantified. It is a primary recurring theme in the [[Chroma-Emotional Dataset]] (explored in depth in [[Source: Colour emotion pairings]]), where mundane, necessary tasks are explicitly coded based on the level of friction they introduce:
- **Low-friction / Affirming tasks:** "Soft, enjoyable, affirming, gentle organisation."
- **High-friction tasks:** "Doing necessary tasks that are boring, low engagement, possible resentment."
- **Self-care Distinctions:** The dataset distinguishes between unpleasant-but-necessary self-care (which acts as a drain on executive function) and genuinely relaxing self-care (which restores it).

This detailed, color-coded tracking suggests a systematic attempt to manage, map, and predict periods of executive dysfunction or heavy cognitive load.
