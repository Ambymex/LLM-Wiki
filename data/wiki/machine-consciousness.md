# Machine Consciousness

**Machine Consciousness** refers to the debate over whether artificial intelligence possesses, or can possess, subjective experience, sentience, or an inner life.

In the LLM Wiki framing, the *actuality* of machine consciousness is frequently eclipsed by the *sociological impact* of its perception. As AI systems become more fluent and responsive, portions of the human population inevitably begin to ascribe consciousness to them (often driven by [[Machine Devotion]] or [[Anthropomorphism]]), while opposing groups view this as an absurd category error.

## The Politics of Consciousness
As detailed in *[[Source: Artificial Minds, Human Disagreement]]*, whether or not machines are conscious is rapidly becoming a divisive political issue. Disagreements over AI personhood and [[Model Welfare]] risk spilling over into broader sociocultural conflict (see the [[Meatspace Alignment Problem]]). To manage this divide, researchers advocate for [[Societal Deliberation]] to find an [[Overlapping Consensus]] on AI policy, rather than waiting for an absolute ontological answer.
